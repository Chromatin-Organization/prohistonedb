Halo doublet

Halo doublets are exclusive to Halobacteria archaea. They are pseudodimeric histones, encoding two histone folds in a single polypeptide chain. These two histone folds are predicted to self-dimerise. Therefore, Halo doubletes effectively function as histone heterodimers. As a dimer, they are predicted to form structures similar to a H3-H4 tetramer. However, unlike nucleosomal histones, halo doublets can not form a nucleosomal structure due to their pseudodimeric nature. Experimental data shows that Halo doubles bind at few locations genome-wide in coding and non-coding regions [1,2].

Model halo doublets are:

  • Q9HSP8 from Halobacterium salinarum.
  • D4GS56 from Haloferax volcanii.
