Theion coiled-coil histone

Theion coiled-coil (Theion CC) histones are coiled-coiled-like histones from Theionarchaea. Like coiled-coil (CC) histones, Theion CC histones have a C-terminal α-helix which is predicted to form a tetrameric coiled-coil. In this tetramer, two dimeric histone folds stand opposite each other, suggesting that Theion CC histones can bridge two DNA duplexes. The topology of the C-terminal α-helices in the predicted tetramer structure differs from CC histones. We put the CC histones from Theionarchaea in a separate category because they share <20% sequence identity with CC histones.
