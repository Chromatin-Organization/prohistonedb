Viral singlet

Viral singlets are histones from megaviruses that contain one histone fold per monomer. These histone folds are similar to the eukaryotic H2A, H2B, H3, and H4 histones. They form nucleosome structures with DNA [1].

Model viral singlets are:

  • A0A3T1CX62, A0A3T1CX47, A0A3T1CXA3, and A0A3T1CWJ9 from Acanthamoeba castellanii medusavirus.
