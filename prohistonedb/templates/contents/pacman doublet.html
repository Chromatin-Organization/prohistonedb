Pacman doublet

Pacman doublets are pseudodimeric histones from Pacman-, Kaumoeba-, and Faustoviruses, encoding two histone folds per polypeptide chain. From the core eukaryotic histones, Pacman histones are most similar to H2A-H2B. They are predicted to form dimers similar to a conventional nucleosomal tetramer. Pacman doublets contain several extensions to their histone fold, including two "arms" and an additional "rear" Î±-helix. They likely bind DNA in a manner similar to a H3-H4 tetramer. The arms are predicted to possibly "grab"/encapsulate DNA.
