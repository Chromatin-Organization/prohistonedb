Nucleosomal histones

Nucleosomal histones are the most common histone type in prokaryotes. They only appear in archaea and are found in almost all phyla except for B1Sed10-29, although only one proteome of this phylum is available in UniProt. Nucleosomal histones are defined by their dimer-dimer interface, which allows them to form a superhelix. In eukaryotes, this superhelix is limited to being an octamer, while in archaea it can theoretically extend infinitely. This archaeal nucleosome structure is known as a hypernucleosome and can be formed by homodimers. The model histones of this category are HMfA/B from Methanothermus fervidus and HTkA/B from Thermococcus kodakarensis [1-13].
