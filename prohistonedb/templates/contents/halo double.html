Halo double histone

Halo double histones are exclusive to Halobacteria. They are double histones, meaning that the monomer contains two histone folds. These two histone folds are predicted to self-dimerise. Therefore, Halo doubles effectively function as histone heterodimers. In the dimer prediction, halo doubles form a structure identical to the nucleosomal tetramer. However, they can not form a nucleosomal superhelix because of their heterodimeric properties. Therefore, Halo doubles likely strongly bend DNA as a dimer. Experimental data shows that Halo doubles bind at few locations genome-wide in coding and non-coding regions [1].
