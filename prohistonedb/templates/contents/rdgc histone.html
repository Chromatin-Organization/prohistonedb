RdgC histone

RdgC histones are a rare histone type found in some Halobacteria and Bacillus (meta)genomes. They are defined by a long C-terminal α-helix and their synteny. Like coiled-coil histones, the C-terminal α-helix is predicted to a tetrameric coiled-coil. In this tetramer, two dimeric histone folds stand opposite each other, suggesting that RdgC histones can bridge two DNA duplexes. RdgC histones are always found in an operon that also contains a RdgC-like protein and an unknown transmembrane protein. The conserved synteny implies a functional relationship between these three proteins. However, what this function entails is unknown. The bacterial RdgC histones, unlike the achaeal ones, often have intrinsically disordered negatively-charged N- and C-terminal tails.
