Coiled-coil histone

Coiled-coil histones are widely distributed throughout archaea and are found in Aenigmatarchaeota, Altiarchaeota, B1Sed10-29, EX4484-52, Iainarchaeota, Methanobacteriota, Micrarchaeota, Nanoarchaeota, Nanohaloarchaeota, and Undinarchaeota. They contain a C-terminal tail which contains a large alpha helix. These C-terminal alpha helices are predicted to form a tetrameric coiled-coil domain. In this tetramer structure, two dimers stand opposite each other and interact through their C-terminal tails. This structure is highly similar to the predicted tetramer structure of Methanococcales histone MJ1647, for which DNA-bridging has been shown experimentally [1]. Therefore, we predict that coiled-coil histones bridge two DNA duplexes, one duplex at each histone dimer. Coiled-coil histones often contain disordered N- and C-terminal tails which are strongly negatively charged. However, these tails are missing in the coiled-coil histone from Methanothermus fervidus, the archeon which contains the model nucleosomal histones HMfA/B.
