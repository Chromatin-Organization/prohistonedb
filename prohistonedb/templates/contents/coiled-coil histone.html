Coiled-coil histone

Coiled-coil histones are widely distributed throughout archaea and are found in Euryarchaeota en DPANN. They contain a C-terminal tail which contains a large α-helix. These C-terminal α-helices are predicted to form a tetrameric coiled-coil domain. In this tetramer structure, two dimeric histone folds stand opposite each other and interact through their C-terminal tails. This allows CC histones to bridge DNA by binding separate DNA duplexes with each histone dimer [1]. The tetramer is similar to the predicted tetramer structure of Methanococcales histone MJ1647, which also bridges DNA [2]. Coiled-coil histones often contain disordered N- and C-terminal tails which are strongly negatively charged. However, these tails are missing in the coiled-coil histone from Methanothermus fervidus, the archeon which contains the model nucleosomal histones HMfA/B.

Model CC histones are:

  • HMfC from Methanothermus fervidus.
