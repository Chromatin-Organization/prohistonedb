Methanococcales histone

Methanococcales (Mc) histones are exclusively found in Methanococcales archaea. They contain a C-terminal tail with two alpha helices. These C-terminal tails are predicted by AlphaFold2 to interact in the tetramer structure. In this tetramer structure, two histone dimers stand opposite each other and interact through their C-terminal tails. The tetramer prediction suggests that Mc histones might bridge two DNA duplexes, one at each histone dimer. This ability to bridge DNA has been confirmed experimentally for histone MJ1647 from Methanococcus jannaschii [1].
