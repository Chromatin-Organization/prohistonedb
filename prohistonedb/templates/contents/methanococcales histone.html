Methanococcales histone

Methanococcales (Mc) histones are exclusively found in Methanococcales archaea. They are defined by an additional C-terminal α-helix which together with the α3 helix facilitates tetramerization. In this tetramer structure, two dimeric histone fold stand opposite each other and interact through their C-terminal tails. This tetramer bridges DNA by binding separate DNA duplexes at each dimeric histone fold [1].
