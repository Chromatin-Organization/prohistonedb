Bacterial H2A;H2B

Bacterial H2A;H2B histones are exclusive to Streptomyces bacteria. Bacterial H2A;H2B are doubles histones as they contain two histone folds in the monomer. These two histone folds self dimerise to form a structure identical to the eukaryotic H2A;H2B heterodimer. As sequence identity is low between bacterial H2A;H2B histones and eukaryotic H2A;H2B, we do not think these are contaminations.

Interestingly, bacterial H2A;H2B histones can be clustered into three different groups which share little sequence identity. How Streptomyces acquired these eukaryotic-like proteins and why there is so much sequence divergence between some bacterial H2A;H2B histones even though they are from very closely related organism is unknown. As the bacterial H2A;H2B histones diverge so much from their eukaryotic counterparts, it is unclear if they bind DNA. Most bacterial H2A;H2B, however, do contain positively charged a1 faces. Furthermore, they also contain a long N-terminal tail and some contain the acidic patch. A bacterial H2A;H2B is present in the model organism Streptomyces coelicolor (strain ATCC BAA-471 / A3(2) / M145).
