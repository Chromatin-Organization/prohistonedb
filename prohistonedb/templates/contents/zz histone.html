ZZ histone

ZZ histones are part of the a3 superfamily as they contain a truncated a3 helix. They are found in the bacteria Bdellovibrionota (class Bdellovibrionia), and Myxococcota. ZZ histones contain a bacterial dimer-like histone fold on their N-terminus and a ZZ-type zinc finger on their C-terminus. This ZZ-domain has two zinc-binding sites and is structurally similar to the ZZ-domains of eukaryotic proteins HERC2, p300, and ZZZ3. The histone-fold most likely binds DNA in a similar manner to the bacterial dimer histones since sequence identity is high between the two. However, the role of the ZZ-domain is unknown.
