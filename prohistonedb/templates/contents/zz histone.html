ZZ histone

ZZ histones are Î±3 histones histones from Bdellovibrionota (class Bdellovibrionia), and Myxococcota. A bacterial dimer-like histone fold is on the C-terminus, and a ZZ-type zinc finger is on the N-terminus. The ZZ-type zinc finger domain has two zinc binding sites (C2H2 and C4) and is structurally similar to the ZZ-domains of eukaryotic proteins HERC2, p300, and ZZZ3. The ZZ-domain is predicted to bind to DNA. The histone-fold most likely binds DNA in a similar manner to the bacterial dimer histones since sequence identity is high between the two.
