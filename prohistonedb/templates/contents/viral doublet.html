Viral doublet

Viral doublets are pseudodimeric histones from megaviruses, encoding two histone folds per monomer. Generally, viral doublets encode eukaryotic-like H2A and H2B or H3 and H4 histone folds together into one monomeric protein. Two viral doublets encoding H2A-H2B and two encoding H3-H4 from nucleosome structures with DNA [1-4].

Model viral doublets are:

  • A0A097I2B5, A0A097I2D0, and A0A097I1R9 from Melbournevirus.
  • D2XB48, D2XB49, and D2XAH0 from Marseillevirus marseillevirus.
